1. Probability of Electron Occupation

Given:
Energy level E = EF + 3kBT
Find: Occupation probability at (a) T = 0 K, (b) T = 300 K

Solution:

The Fermi-Dirac distribution function is:

f(E) = 1 / (1 + e(E - EF)/kBT)

(a) At T = 0 K:

  • For E > EF, f(E) = 0 (all states above EF are empty)
  • Here, E - EF = 3kBT > 0, so:
f(E) = 0

(b) At T = 300 K:

  • Calculate (E - EF)/(kBT) = 3kBT/(kBT) = 3
  • Substitute into the distribution:
f(E) = 1/(1 + e3) = 1/(1 + 20.0855) = 1/21.0855 ≈ 0.0474
f(E) ≈ 0.0474

2. Variation of Fermi Level with Donor Concentration

Given:
• NC = 3 × 1019 cm-3
• ND range: 1012 to 1013 cm-3
• Complete ionization, room temperature

Solution:

For an n-type semiconductor, the Fermi level (EF) relative to the conduction band edge (EC) is:

EC - EF = kBT ln(NC/ND)

Key values at 300 K:

  • kBT = 0.02585 eV
  • NC = 3 × 1019 cm-3

Calculate for ND values:

ND (cm-3) NC/ND EC - EF (eV)
1012 3 × 107 0.47
1013 3 × 106 0.41

Plot Description:

  • X-axis: log₁₀ ND (from 1012 to 1013)
  • Y-axis: EC - EF (in eV)
  • Trend: As ND increases, EC - EF decreases from 0.47 eV to 0.41 eV
  • Curve: Exponential decay (linear in log scale)
Conclusion: Fermi level moves closer to EC as ND increases

3. Intrinsic Resistivity of Silicon

Given:
• ni = 1.5 × 1016 m-3
• μn = 0.13 m²/V·s, μp = 0.05 m²/V·s
• T = 300 K

Solution:

Intrinsic resistivity (ρi) is given by:

ρi = 1/σi = 1/[q ni (μn + μp)]

Given values:

  • q = 1.6 × 10-19 C
  • μn + μp = 0.13 + 0.05 = 0.18 m²/V·s

Calculation:

σi = (1.6 × 10-19) × (1.5 × 1016) × (0.18) = 4.32 × 10-4 S/m
ρi = 1/(4.32 × 10-4) ≈ 2314.8 Ω·m
ρi ≈ 2315 Ω·m

4. Drift Current Density

Part 1: Derivation

Drift current density due to electrons (Jn) under electric field (ℰ):

  • Force on electron: F = -qℰ
  • Drift velocity: vd = -μnℰ
  • Current density: Jn = -q n vd = q n μn ℰ

For holes: Jp = q p μp ℰ

Total drift current density:
Jdrift = q(n μn + p μp)ℰ

Part 2: Calculation

Given:
• n = 1020 m-3
• μn = 0.1 m²/V·s
• ℰ = 1000 V/m
• Assume p ≈ 0 (n-type dominance)

Solution:

Jn = q n μn ℰ = (1.6 × 10-19) × (1020) × (0.1) × (1000)
Jn = 1600 A/m²
Jdrift = 1600 A/m²

5. Intrinsic Carrier Concentration

Given:
• T = 300 K
• me* = 0.12 m0, mh* = 0.28 m0
• Eg = 0.67 eV

Solution:

Intrinsic carrier concentration:

ni = √(NC NV) e-Eg/2kBT

Step 1: Calculate NC and NV

NC = 2(2πme*kBT/h²)3/2
NV = 2(2πmh*kBT/h²)3/2

Step 2: Use standard formula:

ni² = (NC NV) e-Eg/kBT

Step 3: Substitute values:

  • kBT = 0.02585 eV
  • Eg/(kBT) = 0.67/0.02585 ≈ 25.92
  • Effective density ratio: √(me* mh*)/m0 = √(0.12 × 0.28) ≈ 0.183

Calculation:

ni = 2.5 × 1025 · (0.183)3/2 · e-25.92/2 m-3
ni ≈ 2.5 × 1025 × 0.078 × e-12.96
e-12.96 ≈ 2.33 × 10-6
ni ≈ 2.5 × 1025 × 0.078 × 2.33 × 10-6 ≈ 4.54 × 1018 m-3
ni ≈ 4.54 × 1018 m-3

Summary of Key Formulas Used

  1. Fermi-Dirac: f(E) = 1/(1 + e(E - EF)/kBT)
  2. Fermi Level (n-type): EC - EF = kBT ln(NC/ND)
  3. Resistivity: ρi = 1/[q ni (μn + μp)]
  4. Drift Current: Jdrift = q(nμn + pμp)ℰ
  5. Intrinsic Concentration: ni = √(NC NV) e-Eg/2kBT
